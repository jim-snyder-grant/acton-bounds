## Policy / tone decisions

- **No proposal to replace monuments** in cover notes to neighboring towns.
  Restoring existing monuments is fine to suggest; replacing is not.
- **Do not cite MGL Ch. 42 §2's five-year requirement or the $5 fine** when
  writing to neighboring town clerks or Select Boards. The fine is nominal and
  essentially unenforced; Acton itself hasn't perambulated in many years, so
  leading with the legal requirement would be tone-deaf. It's fine to describe
  *what perambulation is* in plain language without invoking the statute as
  leverage.
- Letters to town clerks requesting their perambulation reports should
  acknowledge that the town may have already sent Acton a copy (per the
  statute's contiguous-town notification requirement) and that a cursory
  search just didn't turn it up — avoids sounding accusatory.
- Cover notes to Select Boards (as opposed to clerk inquiry letters) are a
  separate, friendlier document: talking points + good news (monuments Acton
  found that they hadn't), not a request for records.

## Cover page

### Design

- Letter-size page (8.5 × 11 in), cream background (#f5f2ec)
- Frame of photos around the border: 5 portrait photos per side (left/right),
  5 landscape photos top and bottom
- Photos tilted randomly ±15° (sides) and ±12° (top/bottom), with white border
  and slight overlap; scrapbook/documentary feel
- Center area: Acton town seal, thin gold horizontal rules, title block
- Title typography: Georgia serif throughout; "Acton Bounds Report" bold ~14pt
  equivalent, "2025–2026" lighter, "PERAMBULATION OF TOWN BOUNDS" small gold
  caps below lower rule

### Tool and workflow

- Built with **SimpInkScr** (Simple Inkscape Scripting extension for Inkscape)
- Script: `acton_cover.py` in the Bounds Drive folder
- Photos are pre-downscaled to max 800px via PIL before being passed to
  Inkscape, to keep the exported PDF small (was 145MB at full res, now ~5MB)
- Output is a live Inkscape SVG (all objects editable by hand after running)
- Export to PDF via Inkscape's normal PDF export

### Key SimpInkScr lessons learned

- `image()` places photos at native pixel dimensions — always pre-scale with
  PIL or the images will be enormous on the canvas
- Do NOT use chained `.scale()` then `.translate()` — ordering goes wrong.
  Instead pass a single SVG transform string to `image()`:
  `transform=f'translate({x0},{y0}) scale({scale_factor})'`
  (SVG applies right-to-left: scale fires first, then translate)
- `embed=False` does NOT prevent Inkscape from embedding images when exporting
  to PDF — Inkscape embeds them anyway. Pre-scaling with PIL is the only
  reliable way to control PDF file size.
- Text sizing is approximately 2× what you'd expect from pt values — halve
  all font sizes from typical print specs as a starting point, then tune.
- `canvas.width` / `canvas.height` return values in SVG user units (px at
  96dpi for a letter-size doc: ~816 × 1056).
- Background rect must be drawn FIRST (before any photos) so it sits behind
  everything — SimpInkScr draws in painter's order.

## Open questions / loose ends

  penalty, was repealed in 1973. Current law (§2, checked live) has no
  fine. Both the $5 (1648 law) and $20 (Perambulation Plan.odt) figures
  are historical/stale — neither appears in current law. Legal
  Background draft reflects this.

- Acton/Boxborough Mass Ave (Rte 111) and Acton/Concord Rte 2 (southerly

  neighboring town's records — candidates for the policy recommendations
